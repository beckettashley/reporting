# Offer Insights — Final

Plain-English observations written as testable comparisons: what was tried, how it changed performance, and a one-line why. No strategy jargon ("anchor," "tier," "ladder"), no overwhelming volume/revenue context in the headline.

## GlowSerum Pro × 1 ($47.00) — Sales Page

### Insights

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 1 at $47 instead of $45 decreased take rate by 0.4%** (3.1% vs. 3.5%). The $2 increase crossed a price-sensitivity threshold where meaningfully fewer single-unit buyers converted.

---

## GlowSerum Pro × 2 ($94.00) — Sales Page

### Insights

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $94 instead of $90 decreased take rate by 0.3%** (4.1% vs. 4.4%). The $4 increase crossed a price threshold for this bundle size.

- **Customers who chose GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $94 spent an average of $105** — $11 of additional items at checkout. Mid-bundle buyers showed modest willingness to add small extras on top of the primary purchase.

---

## GlowSerum Pro × 3 ($141.00) — Sales Page

### Insights

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 3 at $141 instead of $135 decreased take rate by 0.1%** (2.6% vs. 2.7%). The price change had minimal effect at this top-tier price, suggesting buyers at this level are selecting for the largest bundle regardless of small price variations.

- **Customers who chose GlowSerum Pro × 3 at $141 spent an average of $159** — $18 of additional items at checkout. Top-bundle buyers showed the largest per-order attach of the GlowSerum sales page options.

---

## GlowSerum Pro × 1 ($45.00) — Sales Page

### Insights

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 1 at $45 instead of $47 increased take rate by 0.4%** (3.5% vs. 3.1%) and generated 57% more orders. The $2 decrease pulled substantially more single-unit buyers, indicating a real price threshold between $45 and $47.

- **Pairing GlowSerum Pro × 1 at $45 with GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $90 as the middle option increased the middle option's take rate to 5.1%** — the highest of any GlowSerum configuration. The clean 2x price relationship made the middle option feel like the best value.

## GlowSerum Pro × 2 ($90.00) — Sales Page

### Insights

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $90 instead of $94 increased take rate by 0.3%** (4.4% vs. 4.1%) and generated 72% more orders. The $4 decrease crossed a price threshold that unlocked substantially more volume.

- **Pairing GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $90 with GlowSerum Pro × 1 at $45 as the cheaper option increased take rate to 5.1%** — the best-performing configuration for this offer. The 2x price step between the two options made the $90 bundle feel like a clear upgrade without being a stretch.

- **Customers who chose GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $90 spent an average of $100** — $10 of additional items at checkout. The attach behavior stayed consistent across every configuration this offer appeared in.

## GlowSerum Pro × 3 ($135.00) — Sales Page

- **Pricing GlowSerum Pro × 2 at $91 instead of $94 on the upsell page increased take rate by 3%** (21.4% vs. 18.4%). Even a small price difference meaningfully shifted conversion at the upsell position.

## Daily Moisturizer × 1 ($26.00) — Upsell Page

### Insights

- **Positioning Daily Moisturizer × 1 at $26 as a post-purchase upsell produced a 26.6% take rate** — the highest upsell rate in the account. The combination of a low price and a natural routine-completion pairing made this near-automatic for skincare buyers.

- **Showing Daily Moisturizer × 1 at $26 after a GlowSerum bundle purchase instead of a single-unit GlowSerum purchase increased take rate by 2x.** Buyers who already committed to a larger skincare purchase were much more likely to complete their routine with an additional product.

- **Showing Daily Moisturizer × 1 at $26 on orders over $200 instead of orders under $150 increased take rate by 12%** (31% vs. 19%). Larger cart sizes showed noticeably less price sensitivity to small add-ons.

## Recovery Kit × 1 ($61.00) — Upsell Page

### Insights

- **Positioning Recovery Kit × 1 at $61 on the upsell page instead of at checkout increased take rate by 9.4%** (18.6% vs. 9.2%). The dedicated upsell page gave buyers the consideration time a $61 item needs — checkout's faster pace didn't support that.

- **Showing Recovery Kit × 1 at $61 after a pillow purchase instead of a skincare purchase increased take rate by 13%** (24% vs. 11%). Sleep and comfort buyers responded strongly to the Recovery Kit's positioning in ways skincare buyers didn't.

## Sleep Mask × 1 ($14.00) — Checkout Page

### Insights

- **Showing Sleep Mask × 1 at $14 on orders over $200 instead of orders under $150 increased take rate by 13%** (31% vs. 18%). Larger cart sizes showed significantly lower price sensitivity to the small checkout add-on.

- **Sleep Mask × 1 at $14 performed strongest as a checkout add-on after pillow purchases**, consistent with buyers completing a sleep-focused basket rather than adding unrelated items.
